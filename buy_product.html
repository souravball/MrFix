What is a refurbished phone?

Refurbished phones are second-hand mobiles that have been either sold or returned due to the original buyer changing their mind or because the phone had a minor fault. They are then repaired and reconditioned extensively so they are in perfect working order. Because they aren’t new, they are cheap phones – but great quality too.

To be classified as refurbished, all second-hand mobile phones are thoroughly checked, repaired, and tested so they work exactly as they would if new.

In the UK, refurbished phones are firmly covered by the Consumer Rights Act (unlike privately sold second-hand phones) so there’s no need to be concerned about the quality of the battery, camera, sensors, speakers, screen – or any other component, for that matter. If your phone isn’t as described, you can return it.

While there might be some minor superficial marks or wear and tear (shown via the ‘grade’ of the listing), you can buy a refurbished phone confident that you’re getting a top-quality second-hand mobile. And for a much lower price than a new handset.

All refurbished phones for sale in the UK have their data wiped thoroughly in line with the Data Protection Act too, and where possible, will have the newest operating system loaded and ready, straight out the box. Most even come with warranties for even greater peace of mind too.

Refurbished phones are a good option if you want to reduce the cost of your mobile phone usage. That’s because they are much cheaper than if you were to buy a handset brand-new.

While they are second-hand, refurbished mobiles are a cut above. They’re checked, tested, and repaired by professional techies with a fine-toothed comb, and come with warranties that you simply won’t get if you see a cheap phone for sale by a private second-hand seller. They typically come unlocked, so you can shop around for low-cost SIM-only contracts too. With no agonising contract lock-in, you’ll be laughing all the way to your banking app.

Plus, a refurbished phone is the eco-friendly choice. Instead of getting a new phone manufactured from fresh materials and shipped tens of thousands of miles, you get a warm and fuzzy feeling from knowing you’re reducing your carbon footprint and fighting climate change.

That said, there are a couple of SE-sized things to keep in mind. First, it’s unlikely you’ll be able to get the very latest phones refurbished – you’ll have to wait a few months until the eager beavers start selling up to fund their next flashy handset. You also might not get a cable, plug adapter, or earphones either – though we’ll let you know if that’s the case.

There can also be a few marks or scuffs on the body too, though you’ll be told exactly what condition the phone body is in before you buy. They can also be a little more expensive than second-hand phones too – technicians deserve a decent wage, after all.

Refurbished phone warranties are also shorter than new phones, and lastly, some insurers can be a bit iffy about cover – though you can usually find a deal by shopping around.

We’re going to say it loud and clear for the folks at the back: refurbished phones are good. Great, even!
